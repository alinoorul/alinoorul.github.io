GENE2WAVE


Susumu Onho converted genes to music. I’m converting DNA sequences to music to do Fourier transforms

on them to obtain features that are easily compared.

I’m converting genetic sequences to waves. Each DNA sequence has a 4 -dimensional wave where each

dimension represents a base. There is a time -like dimension that is used to denote discrete steps.

For example, the A wave has a spike wherever we see A in a DNA sequence. Each DNA sequence is thus a

sum of 4 waves.

Encoding these waves in such a manner that their frequencies differ from each other (A wave spikes have a

different frequency than G wave spikes), then taking a Fourier transform of the sum wave (A+G+T+C) might

make a signature for each DNA sequence.

This signature MAY allow for alignment -free comparison of DNA sequences. Wave interference and phase -

shifting play some role as well. I’m researching this.
